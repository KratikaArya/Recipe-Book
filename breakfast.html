Caesar Salad

A classic Caesar salad is a delightful combination of crisp romaine lettuce, crunchy croutons, and a creamy dressing. Here’s a straightforward recipe to make a delicious Caesar salad from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Croutons

  • ½ French baguette
  • Cut into ¼-inch thick slices
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic (about 2 small cloves)
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • Sea salt, to taste

For the Caesar Salad Dressing

  • 2 small garlic cloves, minced (about 1 teaspoon)
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1½ teaspoons red wine vinegar
  • ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt (or to taste)
  • ⅛ teaspoon black pepper (plus more for serving)
  • ¼ cup finely grated Parmesan cheese

For the Salad

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce (or 2 small heads), chopped
  • Additional grated or shaved Parmesan cheese for topping

Instructions

1. Prepare the Croutons:

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • In a bowl, combine the bread slices with olive oil, minced garlic, and grated Parmesan. Toss until evenly coated.
  • Spread the bread cubes on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake for about 10-12 minutes or until golden and crispy. Set aside to cool.

2. Make the Dressing:

  • In a small bowl, whisk together minced garlic, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, lemon juice, and red wine vinegar.
  • Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously until emulsified.
  • Stir in salt, pepper, and grated Parmesan cheese. Adjust seasoning to taste.

3. Assemble the Salad:

  • In a large salad bowl, combine the chopped romaine lettuce with croutons.
  • Drizzle with Caesar dressing and toss gently to coat the lettuce evenly.
  • Top with additional Parmesan cheese and freshly cracked black pepper before serving.

Tips

  • Make it Ahead: You can prepare the croutons in advance and store them in an airtight container.
  • For Extra Flavor: Consider adding anchovies to your dressing for a traditional touch. Simply mash them into a paste before mixing with other ingredients.
  • Variations: Add grilled chicken or shrimp for added protein, or substitute chickpeas for a vegetarian option.
