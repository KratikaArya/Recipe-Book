Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is typically served with chutney and sambar. Here’s a detailed recipe to prepare dosa from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Dosa Batter

  • 2 cups rice (preferably parboiled or sona masuri)
  • ½ cup urad dal (black gram dal)
  • ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional, for fermentation)
  • 2 tablespoons chana dal (optional, for flavor)
  • ½ cup poha (flattened rice, optional for crispiness)
  • Salt, to taste (add after fermentation)
  • Water, as needed for soaking and blending

Instructions

1. Soak the Ingredients:

  • Rinse the rice and urad dal thoroughly.
  • In a large bowl, soak the rice, urad dal, fenugreek seeds, and chana dal in enough water for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  • If using poha, soak it separately in water for about 30 minutes before blending.

2. Grind the Batter:

  • Drain the soaked ingredients and transfer them to a blender.
  • Add enough water to achieve a thick but pourable consistency. Blend until smooth; the batter should have a slight graininess from the rice.
  • Transfer the batter to a large bowl.

3. Ferment the Batter:

  • Cover the bowl with a lid or cloth and place it in a warm area to ferment for 8-12 hours. The batter should double in volume and have a slightly sour smell.

4. Make Dosas:

  • After fermentation, stir the batter gently. If it's too thick, add a little water to reach your desired consistency.
  • Heat a non-stick skillet or cast iron griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin layer.
  • Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
  • Flip if desired, but traditionally dosas are served only on one side.

5. Serve:

  • Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or sambar.

Tips

  • Make it Ahead: You can prepare the batter in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • For Extra Flavor: Add spices like cumin or black pepper to the batter before cooking for added flavor.
  • Variations: Experiment with different types of lentils or add finely chopped vegetables to the batter for unique dosas.
