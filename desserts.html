Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made from khoya (milk solids) that are shaped into balls, fried, and soaked in sugar syrup. It's sweet, rich, and perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients

For Gulab Jamun

  • 200 grams khoya (mawa)
  • 50 grams all-purpose flour (maida)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • Ghee or oil for frying

For Sugar Syrup

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • A few cardamom pods (crushed)
  • A few drops of rose water (optional)

Instructions

1. Prepare Sugar Syrup:

  • In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and crushed cardamom. Bring to a boil until sugar dissolves completely.
  • Lower heat and simmer for about 5 minutes. Add rose water if using. Set aside to cool.

2. Make Gulab Jamun Balls:

  • Crumble khoya in a mixing bowl and mix in all-purpose flour and baking soda until well combined.
  • Knead gently to form a soft dough. Divide into small portions and roll into smooth balls without cracks.

3. Fry Gulab Jamuns:

  • Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan over low-medium heat.
  • Add balls in batches and fry until golden brown on all sides. Ensure that the oil is not too hot to avoid burning them.

4. Soak in Syrup:

  • Once fried, immediately transfer them to warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least an hour before serving.

Tips

  • Syrup Consistency: Ensure that the sugar syrup is warm when adding the fried balls for better absorption.
  • Add Flavor: You can add saffron strands or rose essence to the syrup for enhanced flavor.
  • Making Ahead: You can prepare the syrup a day in advance; just warm it up before adding the gulab jamun.
